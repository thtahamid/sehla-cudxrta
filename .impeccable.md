## Design Context

### Users
Traffic operations analysts, mobility planners, venue operators, and technical stakeholders who need a fast operational picture of City Walk Dubai's surrounding road network, signal state, lane allocation, and traffic pressure.

### Brand Personality
Precise, calm, and operational. The interface should feel like a professional transport control surface: readable under pressure, data-dense without clutter, and explicit about live data provenance.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the Sequence dashboard style: dark fixed navigation frame, light data canvas, restrained emerald/teal accents, compact cards, tables, and controls. Motion is functional: moving flow markers, signal phase changes, and subtle loading/entrance states. Avoid marketing pages, decorative hero sections, heavy shadows, glow effects, and playful animation.

### Design Principles
- Show the working network first; explanatory text stays secondary and source-focused.
- Distinguish sourced infrastructure from simulated live flow so operators can trust the model.
- Keep controls compact and repeat-use friendly.
- Use motion to communicate traffic movement and signal timing, with reduced-motion support.
- Preserve dense geospatial detail while keeping labels and lane metadata scannable.
